Passes and Assists

 These designs visualize the spatial distribution of passes and assists over the pitch. Assists in this context refer to passes right before a shot attempt, whether the attempt leads to a goal or not. The pitch is a regulation-sized 120-by-80 yard field. The selected team's direction of play is to the right.

Assists Heatmap

 An assist is commonly understood to be the last pass to the scorer of a goal. Each assist is denoted by a ring drawn on the pitch.

The pitch is divided into a 6x3 grid commonly used by coaches (see for example, Zone 14) with the central strip as wide as the six yard box. Each cell of the grid represents a zone.

The color intensity of a zone represents the frequency of assists that orginated from that zone.
